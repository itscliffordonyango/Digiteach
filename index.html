About the Initiative

Access is not enough.
The real goal is confidence.

The Digital Confidence Initiative helps teachers and communities move from digital access to digital confidence through practical training, online safety education and community-led support.

Learn More

What Teachers Learn

4 Practical Areas of Confidence

Plain-language skills teachers can use the same day— in the classroom, school administration and everyday life.

Learn More
Area 01

Digital Foundations

Computer basics, typing, files, folders, saving documents, USB devices and printing.

Area 02

Internet & Email

Browsers, Gmail, passwords, downloads, uploads, Google Drive and cloud storage.

Area 03

Teacher Productivity

Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, lesson planning, reports and attendance.

Area 04

Digital Safety

Scams, phishing, misinformation, password hygiene, fake links and safe browsing.

Why This Matters

Access is growing.
Confidence hasn't kept pace.

Access

Phones, computers, internet access and digital services are becoming increasingly available. Unfortunately, many people still lack the confidence to use them independently.

  • I have a laptop but I'm afraid to touch it.
  • I don't know which websites are safe.
  • I always forget my passwords.

Confidence

The same devices become practical tools for teaching, administration and everyday life.

  • Creates lesson notes confidently.
  • Uses email and cloud storage safely.
  • Helps learners and fellow teachers.
